What distinguishes a loud sound from a soft sound, or a high-pitched sound from a low-pitched sound? Waves of the same type can have certain different characteristics.

Waves have an amplitude $A$, the maximum displacement of a particle in the wave. The wavelength $\lambda$ of a wave is the length between repeating sections of a wave, i.e. the distance between the peaks or troughs of the wave. The frequency $f$ of a wave represents the number of wavelengths that pass a certain point in unit time.

The velocity of a wave is the distance the wave travels per time. Since the wave travels one wavelength in one period $T = \frac{1}{f}$, \( v = \frac{\text{one wavelength}}{\text{one period}} = \frac{\lambda}{T} = f \lambda . \)
