Wave Interference

When two waves of the same type have the same frequency, we say that they are coherent.

There's still one property we can change for two waves with the same frequency, and that is their phase difference $\phi$. That represents the fraction of a cycle one wave is ahead of the other by.

For instance, if wave two is half a cycle ($\pi$ radians) ahead of wave one, the peaks of wave two will match up with the troughs of wave one. The resultant displacement is zero, and the two waves cancel each other out. This is known as destructive interference.

On the other hand, if wave two is a whole number of cycles ($0,2\pi,4\pi,...$ radians) ahead of wave one, the peaks of wave two will match up with the peaks of wave one. The resultant wave has an amplitude $A=A_1+A_2$. This is known as constructive interference.
