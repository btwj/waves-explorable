Examples of Waves

There are many different examples of transverse and longitudinal waves in nature.

One example of a transverse wave is light. Light is an electromagnetic wave, and so the values of the electric and magnetic fields oscillate perpendicularly to the direction of light.

One example of a longitudinal wave is sound. Sound occurs due to the oscillations of air molecules. There are alternating periods of high pressure, known as compression, and periods of low pressure, known as rarefaction.
